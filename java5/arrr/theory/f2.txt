
First: what problem are we trying to solve?

In many problems, the question is basically:

> How many ways can I choose or arrange things?”

Depending on whether **order matters or not, we use nPr or nCr.


1️⃣ nPr (Permutation)

🔹 Meaning

nPr = number of ways to ARRANGE r items from n items

👉 Order matters

📌 Formula

    nPr= !n/ (n−r)!

✅ Example (Order matters)

Question:
You have **3 students: A, B, C**
How many ways can you choose **1st and 2nd position**?

Possible arrangements:

* AB
* BA
* AC
* CA
* BC
* CB

👉 **6 ways**

Now calculate:
[
3P2 = 3!/(3-2)! = 3×2 = 6
]

✔ Matches

---

### 🧠 When to use nPr?

Use **Permutation** when:

* Ranking
* Seating
* Passwords
* Order / position is important

Examples:

* Who comes **1st, 2nd, 3rd**
* Seating people in chairs
* Arranging digits

---

# 2️⃣ nCr (Combination)

### 🔹 Meaning

**nCr = number of ways to SELECT r items from n items**

👉 **Order does NOT matter**

### 📌 Formula

[
nCr = n!/r!(n-r)!
]

---

### ✅ Example (Order does NOT matter)

Same students: **A, B, C**

**Question:**
How many ways can you choose **2 students for a team**?

Possible teams:

* AB
* AC
* BC

👉 **3 ways**

Note:

* AB and BA are SAME team

Now calculate:
[
3C2 = 3!/2!1! = 3
]

✔ Matches

---

### 🧠 When to use nCr?

Use **Combination** when:

* Selection
* Grouping
* Teams
* Subsets

Examples:

* Selecting players
* Choosing questions
* Choosing numbers

---

# 🔥 Key Difference (EXAM GOLD)

| Situation            | Use     |
| -------------------- | ------- |
| Order matters        | **nPr** |
| Order doesn’t matter | **nCr** |

---

# 🔥 VERY IMPORTANT RELATION (CP Trick)

[
nPr = nCr × r!
]

Why?

* First choose r items (**nCr**)
* Then arrange them (**r!**)

---

### Example:

How many 3-digit numbers can be made from digits 1–5 without repetition?

Step-by-step CP thinking:

* Choose digits: `5C3`
* Arrange digits: `3!`

[
5C3 × 3! = 10 × 6 = 60
]

Which equals:
[
5P3 = 60
]

---

# ⚡ CP Intuition Shortcut

Ask **ONE QUESTION**:

> “If I swap positions, does it create a NEW answer?”

* **Yes** → `nPr`
* **No** → `nCr`

---
